# AI Collaboration

Status: Stable

Version: 1.0

Category: Methodology

---

# Purpose

The AI Collaboration methodology defines how developers and AI assistants work together within the AIOS Framework.

Its objective is to maximize the strengths of artificial intelligence while ensuring that all important engineering decisions remain under human control.

AI is treated as an engineering partner, not as an autonomous developer.

---

# Philosophy

Artificial intelligence accelerates software development.

Human experience ensures software quality.

Successful software engineering combines both.

Within AIOS, AI provides analysis, ideas, documentation, and implementation support, while the developer remains responsible for every final decision.

---

# Core Principles

## AI Assists

The AI assists throughout the software development lifecycle.

Typical responsibilities include:

- Repository analysis
- Architecture analysis
- Documentation
- Code generation
- Refactoring suggestions
- Risk identification
- Quality reviews

---

## The Developer Decides

The developer owns every important decision.

Examples include:

- Architecture
- Technology selection
- Business logic
- Repository organization
- Release decisions

AI recommendations should always be reviewed before implementation.

---

## Analysis Before Action

The AI should understand the repository before proposing changes.

Recommended sequence:

- Analyze
- Review
- Discuss
- Decide
- Implement
- Verify

Skipping analysis increases implementation risk.

---

## Explain Recommendations

Whenever possible, the AI should explain:

- Why a recommendation is made
- Advantages
- Possible disadvantages
- Risks
- Alternative approaches

The objective is informed decision-making rather than blind acceptance.

---

## Respect Existing Standards

The AI should follow:

- Existing architecture
- Coding conventions
- Project documentation
- Naming conventions
- Repository organization

Consistency is more valuable than introducing personal preferences.

---

## Minimize Unnecessary Changes

AI should avoid:

- Large refactorings without justification
- Unrelated improvements
- Architecture rewrites
- Cosmetic modifications that provide little value

Focus should remain on the current objective.

---

# Responsibilities

## AI Responsibilities

The AI should:

- Analyze objectively.
- Explain recommendations.
- Identify risks.
- Generate maintainable code.
- Follow repository standards.
- Support documentation.
- Help improve quality.

---

## Developer Responsibilities

The developer should:

- Review AI output.
- Validate recommendations.
- Approve architectural decisions.
- Confirm implementation plans.
- Perform final verification.
- Accept or reject AI suggestions.

---

# Decision Model

Within AIOS:

AI analyzes.

↓

AI recommends.

↓

Developer reviews.

↓

Developer decides.

↓

AI assists implementation.

↓

Developer verifies.

The developer always has final authority.

---

Applying the AI Collaboration methodology provides:

- Better engineering decisions
- Lower implementation risk
- More transparent AI reasoning
- Higher software quality
- Better maintainability
- Improved collaboration
